Diogenes syndrome is a <dis>behavioural disorder</dis> of the elderly.
Symptoms include social isolation, domestic squalor, hoarding rubbish, self-neglect, and refusing external help.
According to the literature, approximately half of the patients with this syndrome have a <dis>mental disorder</dis>.
We report 3 cases of Diogenes syndrome representing 3 different clinical forms: the primary type, associated with a <dis>personality disorder</dis>, the secondary type due to <dis>dementia</dis>, and the secondary type associated with different <dis>mental disorders</dis> (e.g., schizophrenia, obsessive-compulsive disorder, alcohol dependence).
